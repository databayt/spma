## Organizational Structure

The Sudanese Project Management Association operates under a democratic governance model with clear separation of responsibilities across its organizational bodies.

### Board of Directors

The Board of Directors is the highest governing authority of SPMA, responsible for strategic direction, policy development, and oversight of association activities. The board consists of elected members who serve two-year terms.

**Current Board Composition:**

- **President** — Chief executive responsible for overall leadership and external representation
- **Secretary General & Rapporteur** — Manages administrative operations and official communications
- **Financial Secretary** — Oversees budgeting, financial reporting, and resource allocation
- **Media Secretary** — Leads public relations, marketing, and digital presence
- **Scientific Researcher** — Coordinates research initiatives and knowledge development
- **Professional Development Secretary** — Manages training programs and certification pathways
- **Planning & Programs Secretary** — Oversees strategic planning and program execution
- **Membership & Volunteers Secretary** — Handles member services and volunteer coordination
- **External Relations Secretary** — Manages partnerships and international affiliations

### Committees

SPMA organizes its work through specialized committees:

- **Training & Development Committee** — Designs and delivers professional development programs
- **Events & Conferences Committee** — Plans and executes conferences, seminars, and workshops
- **Research & Publications Committee** — Produces research papers, case studies, and newsletters
- **Membership Committee** — Reviews applications and manages member engagement
- **Standards & Ethics Committee** — Ensures adherence to professional standards and ethical guidelines

## Bylaws

### Article 1: Name and Purpose

The association shall be known as the Sudanese Project Management Association (SPMA). Its purpose is to promote the practice, science, and profession of project management throughout Sudan.

### Article 2: Membership

Membership is open to all individuals and organizations interested in advancing project management in Sudan. Members shall abide by the association's code of ethics and professional conduct.

### Article 3: Elections

Board elections are held every two years. All active members in good standing are eligible to vote and run for board positions. Elections are conducted through a transparent, democratic process.

### Article 4: Meetings

The association holds a General Assembly meeting annually. The Board of Directors meets monthly to review progress and make decisions on operational matters.

### Article 5: Amendments

Bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of the General Assembly. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing at least 30 days before the vote.
