## About SPMA

The **Sudanese Project Management Association (SPMA)** is a voluntary, nonprofit professional association dedicated to spreading the culture of project management in Sudan. Founded to bridge the gap between international project management standards and local practice, SPMA serves as the leading voice for project management professionals across the country.

## Mission

To promote and advance the practice, science, and profession of project management throughout Sudan by providing access to knowledge, certification pathways, networking opportunities, and professional development resources.

## Vision

A Sudan where every project is managed professionally, delivering value to organizations and communities through the consistent application of global best practices in project management.

## Key Objectives

- **Knowledge Dissemination** — Spread project management culture through workshops, seminars, and training programs
- **Professional Development** — Support practitioners in obtaining internationally recognized certifications such as PMP, CAPM, and PMI-ACP
- **Networking** — Connect project management professionals across industries and sectors in Sudan
- **Standards Advocacy** — Promote the adoption of PMI standards and methodologies in Sudanese organizations
- **Community Building** — Create a vibrant community of practice for project managers at all levels

## Programs

SPMA offers a range of programs to support the project management community:

### Training Programs
Structured courses covering the PMBOK Guide, agile methodologies, risk management, and specialized topics tailored to the Sudanese context.

### Certification Support
Study groups, exam preparation workshops, and mentorship programs for PMI certification candidates.

### Events
Regular meetups, annual conferences, and guest speaker sessions featuring local and international project management experts.

### Publications
Research papers, case studies, and newsletters highlighting project management trends and success stories from Sudan.
